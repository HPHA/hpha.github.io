  • A vote was taken to replace the entrance sign with a large engraved boulder, there was a consensus to proceed with the new boulder. The existing sign at the entrance was installed when the roads were completed which needs repair. Cost to refinish/repaint/replace wood is up to $5,000; new boulder is estimated to be $4,500. Permit from Township was approved. Board is looking for electrical volunteer to rewire lights once the boulder is installed. Price estimate to include a foundation under the boulder.
  • Road SAD – In 2019 Harrison Twp. had roads assessed by Road Commission. We are looking to have all roads resurfacing next year if the water levels remain low to include Venetian. If the SAD is approved by homeowners the SAD will include a bike shoulder on both sides of Lakeshore for safety purposes. County will only install shoulder if it is done on both sides. Lakeshore is treacherous to walk or ride a bicycle on. Lakeshore is the only stripped road in the subdivision, therefore, it’s the only one that can have bike shoulders. Must obtain 51% of homeowners to agree in order to proceed. The total cost will be divided by number of houses/residence.

    The SAD will Include all streets except W. Archer in the event W. Archer would like to add a pump station. If they vote to add a pump station W. Archer will have their own SAD.

    The board will request assistance from Macomb County Road commission to help supplement the cost once we have a SAD approved. Macomb County will never repave the roads even if the roads fall apart; cost of repaving is upon homeowners to pay for. The County sealed the roads once but will not do again, only patch.

    Neither brick mailboxes nor landscaping will be replaced, only sod will be replaced. The bike path/shoulder will consist of the same type of foundation as the road. *Note the bike path/shoulder IS NOT a parking area, if FEDEX has a delivery they may park over the bike path momentarily.

    Comment was made regarding the end of East Archer needs work which is an abutments. President Mulder brought this issue up to the County Commissioner to add it into their future budget; not much success but Ken will continue to pursue. Comment was made regarding adding speed bumps – President Mulder advised speed bumps are illegal, though we may replace speed sign with electronic speed signs. The Board asks all homeowners to be courteous to walkers and bikers and give them space.
    • In 2018 an estimate of $3,000 per parcel to mill & fill which consists of remove asphalt surface, use existing to create new bed.
    • When the roads were repaved previously it was assessed by frontage as the project included adding drainage.
    • We’ll need to have engineering come out and reassess the roads to get an exact cost by parcel.
    • Bike path will be the full length of Lakeshore Dr. with 3’ shoulder on each side. Stripped & designated. Road commission will only do it if we do both sides. Walk with traffic, ride against traffic.
    • Residence cannot fill in swales or park cars on the swales. The weight crushes storm sewer. Thirty years ago, the township assessed fines to individuals who parked on swales.
  • New Harrison Township residential ordinances which will affect residence in Huron Point. Recreation vehicle storage has changed, to review the changes review the bottom of the Harrison Township website. Provides info on what can be stored, how can be stored, and setbacks.
    • Empty lot – lot owner must own what is stored on the lot
  • Huron Pointe Excavating – Request a permit from EAGLE and Army COR to construct a wave attenuation, the board submitted an objection to the project on behalf of all HPHA homeowners. requested a permit from EAGLE and Army COR to object to the project. Huron Pointe Excavating is not using the property in accordance with zoning usage. Property zoned waterfront not for marine usage. They are storing barges is violation.
    • President Mulder recommended homeowners to attend Harrison Township board meeting which are open meeting and voice your objection.
  • Township provided guidance on how to dispose of sandbags. Though the townships recommendation is to leave them in place. If you do not want to leave them in place you CANNOT dump the sand into the canal or at seawall. Please do not dump at prior location where there was sand. Township has 2 designated locations, must dump sand into the sand pile and dispose of bags properly.
  • Discussion was held regarding the Interest in dredging canals, 51% of homeowners must agree. Would be paid for via a SAD, only homeowners who live on the canal would pay.
    • Mechanical dredging vs. vacuuming – remove all the loose stuff, slightly more expensive. Must stay 5’ away from seawall.
    • Need to stop weed growth by dredging not just treating and killing for them to grow back the following year.
  • No subdivision cleanup on June 12, doesn’t appear to be required at this time.
  • Open the floor up for questions and there weren’t any.

 

November, 2018

  • There are funds left over that were collected for the canal weed control treatments before the SAD was enacted. There were several uses proposed for the funds but the consensus was that they should go towards canal improvements and not into the HPHA general fund. Some of the suggestions included cleaning out the culverts at the end of some of the canals to promote better water flow and the addition of lit "NO WAKE" buoys at the entrance to the canals off the Black Creek and Campua Bay. We are looking into the cost and any approvals that must be obtained to make these happen.
  • There was a possibility of having parking spaces added at Aune Park but the feedback we received indicated that this was not a necessity and that people can park on the road around the park when using it.
  • We just completed year 2 of the 5 year SAD for the weed control treatments and while most people saw the improvement from the past when the canals were not treated there were still some problem areas. Please contact a board member if you have concerns with the effectiveness so we can reach out to the contractor. We are going to ask the contractor to attend a meeting in the spring to address some of the technical questions that we cannot not answer. We will also look into getting quotes from contractors used in some of the other subdivisions near us to make sure we are getting the best deal and the most effective treatment when we look at the next SAD.
  • There was not as much information on road resurfacing as we would have liked to have provided, the Township Clerk was bogged down by the recent election but should be free to meet with us shortly. The township had their engineer evaluate the roads so that he can provide us with his recommended solutions moving forward. We still intend to address the bike/walking lane along Lakeshore Dr and the need for better drainage on W Archer and Venetian. When we have more information we will pass it along.
  • We are looking at adding a book sharing box/house near or in the Huron Pointe. The 2 locations that are being looked at are at the county park next to North Star Sail Club or at Aune Park.
  • As speeding continues to be an issue we have still been looking into solar powered radar speed signs strategically placed throughout the sub. The opinions on whether or not they will be effective for the cost seem to be pretty divided.
  • Nanette and Clyde Morency continue to put up great seasonal decorations at the entrances and if you would be willing to volunteer to help out please let us know.
  • At our next meeting in January we will hold elections for the HPHA board if anyone is interested in taking a role on the board.

 

January, 2018

  • 2018 HPHA dues of $25 are due, the form to pay was on the flyer sent out in the mail or is available on www.HPHA.us. We have 460 members on this page and only about 150 residents pay the $25 dues out of roughly 900 properties. The more people that pay their dues the more money the HPHA can put into improvements and events.
  • 2018 HPHA elections were held, we had no new volunteers or nominations. The board members stayed the same but Larry Gass is the new Vice President and Joe Raymon asked to step down but stay on as a Trustee
  • Pardee bridge update from the MCDR. The geotechnical field work has been completed. The seawall design is being finalized. Permits from the MDEQ and FAA have been aquired and the USACE permit will come in after the seawall design is complete. Consumers Energy has cut and capped their line on both sides of the bridge and retired the main attached to the bridge. DTE will be de-energizing and removing their lines over the canal this month. The project will be advertised in February and is scheduled to begin in March.
  • The seawall at the end of Archer Dr is in need of repair/replacement but will not be done with the Pardee bridge project. There are multiple seawalls on County property in Harrison Township in similar condition and they will be bundled together as one project to be added to the budget items.
  • We are still trying to get the ball rolling on road resurfacing and adding a walking/biking shoulder to Lakeshore Dr. We have been working with County Commissioner Robert Leonetti to get an engineer out from the MCDR to give us an assessment of the road conditions and options for repair. While it seems like we have been going around in circles with the MCDR we are going to work with the Township Clerk to start the SAD process to satisfy the MCDR requirements and move forward. As some point we will need volunteers to help poll the neighborhood with options and then get signatures for a SAD if we move forward.
  • We received an update from the USACE on the wetalnds project at the Harley Ensign DNR site. The dredging and material placement in the bay is complete. There is still some remaining work left to be done including tree removal, filling in the research ponds, site grading and planting the entire site including the wetalnds. All work must be complete by the end of May and then there will be a year of minor establishment activities.
  • The bridge on South River Rd that has been reduced to one lane will stay that way for a while. The outside I-beams are failing but the interior ones are fine which is why they are moving the traffic to the center of the bridge. Right now there has not been money budgeted for repair.
  • We will be getting quotes on refurbishing the sign at the entrance that has some problems with wood rotting and in need of new paint.
  • We discussed upcoming events for the year and are looking for volunteers to help organize some of the events. The Huron Pointe garage sale will be June 1-3, we will work with Tony Zadonia to have the 4th Annual HPHA Walleye Tournament, the Huron Pointe Yacht Club would like to work with us on combining efforts for a dinghy/golf cart/bike poker or fun run, and organizing a HPHA raft off on the lake.

 

August, 2017

  • - The weed control SAD is all set for this year and the next 4 years for every canal except for the Venetian canal. There are some problem areas that we are letting the provider know about, we are also seeing if the chemicals can be adjusted since the eel grass seems to be here earlier than previous years. If you have concerns about the treatment of an area let us know.
  • The bridge SAD is still not finalized but Larry Tomenello let us know that another federal grant was secured to apply towards the bridges. The final cost should be much lower than the original estimates. When the amount is finalized the township will send out letters explaining the cost and payment options, I will also repost it when I find out.
  • There has not been a reason determined for the failure of some of the pump stations during the heavy rain last month. The maintanance crews are notified via radio signal when there is a problem with the pumps but if you notice any problems please contact the MCDR. It's getting later in the year but in the event of flooding conditions the township and county have an emergency plan in place to bring out sand and sandbags for Huron Pointe. The residents are responsible for filling and placement.
  • There has been very little interest in the dinghy poker run this year. We will try to change the event to a neighborhood meetup at one of the bays on the lake if people are interested in that. Please let us know.
  • We are currently working with the MCDR and Rob Lionetti to get options and pricing for road resurfacing through out Huron Pointe. This is planning for the future and would not be something that was rushed through without the proper planning and exploration. Items such as a bike path on Lakeshore Dr and drainage solutions for W Archer and Venetian are also being looked at.
  • The Pardee bridge was offiically closed to traffic, the MCDR fiscal year starts on October 1st and once their budget for the year is approved we hope to have a better timeline for the removal of the bridge. We will keep you updated with any information we find out.
  • We are asking the MCDR to look into the deteriorating seawall at the end of E Archer on the Riverdale/Powers canal, there is also a small sinkhole on Lakeshore Dr we have asked them to look at. It was mentioned that when the pump stations were installed there were supposed to be extra pumps purchased for replacement and we will try to find out the history with those.
  • Speeding continues to be an issue, there have been many suggestions to slow people down. Speed bumps in any form have been denied the MCDR, traffic calming islands are an options when the roads are resurfaced, radar speed signs to alert people of their speed and additional stop signs placed throughout Huron Pointe. We are looking into these any any other possibilities that people suggest. If you are concerned with the speeders let the Sheriffs Department know, the more they hear from residents the more likely we are to have the Sheriffs out here.
  • There has been some crimes reported in Huron Pointe and the surrounding area, most seem to be crimes of opportunity. Don't give criminals the chance, make sure your doors and cars are locked, keep you garage doors closed if you're not outside, don't leave items outside and look out for eachother. Report anything suspicious to the Sheriffs Department. We are also looking into cameras for the neighborhood.

 

April, 2017

  • The garage sale is a little over a month away and will be on June 2-4
  • The 3rd Annual Walleye Tournament will be on June 10th, please contact Tony Zadonia for information
  • We are continuing to collect signatures for the Weed Treatment SAD, look for the street captain signs around the sub to stop in and sign or to help volunteer collecting signatures
  • The bridge project engineer Scott W. from the Macomb County Department of Roads was in attendance to talk about the Pardee bridge removal. He confirmed that the bridge will be removed and is budgeted in the MCDR FY18 budget that begins October 1st, 2017. He said he plans to start making preparations for the removal in the fall and when the FY18 budget is approved by the Macomb County Board of Commissioners the removal will be scheduled. His intention is to make this one of the first projects in FY18 because he likes to get the smaller projects out of the way first and it will not require concrete work or other things that are weather dependent.
  • We will begin to look at another SAD for road resurfacing soon because of the time it takes to get everything in place and actually start construction (1-2 years)
